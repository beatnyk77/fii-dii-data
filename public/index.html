Sector-Wise FII / FPI Allocation

Source: NSDL Fortnightly FPI Reports · BSE 22-Sector Classification · via Screener.in (C-MOTS) · Updated twice a month
ℹ️
How is this data calculated? SEBI mandates all Foreign Portfolio Investors (FPIs) to hold securities via Indian depositories (NSDL / CDSL). Every fortnight, depositories publish sector-wise Assets Under Custody (AUC). The % of AUM shown is each sector's share of the total FPI equity portfolio in India. Sectors are classified using BSE's Common Industry Classification. Flows show net FPI inflows/outflows per sector over the last 15-day reporting window and the rolling 12-month period.

Dashboard User Manual & Methodology
A comprehensive end-to-end guide on reading the flow indicators, understanding F&O position signals, and decoding the complex algorithms calculating Market Strength.

1. FII / DII Home Dashboard

The home dashboard provides an immediate snapshot of the primary liquidity flowing into or out of the Indian stock market. It relies on standard Daily Cash Market volume figures released by the NSE.

Market Strength Gauge Aggregates the last 5 days of data, weighting FII Cash, DII Absorption, and Net Liquidity to generate a 0-100 score. A score <30 is a "Bear Regime" prioritizing defense, while >60 is a "Bull Regime" signaling strong institutional accumulation.
Stock Strength Meter A 10-day analytical model factoring in FII/DII historical averages, momentum (ratio of positive to negative flow days), and absorption capacity. Predicts the short-to-medium term bias of the broader indices.
Streak & Velocity Tracking Monitors exactly how many consecutive days FIIs or DIIs have been buying/selling, allowing you to instantly visualize prolonged trends and identify exhaustion points in institutional capitulation.
Flow Heatmaps & Activity GitHub-style daily block rendering to track activity intensity over 45 days. Darker green blocks represent daily buys > ₹5,000 Cr, while darker red signifies heavy distribution > ₹5,000 Cr.

🎲 2. F&O Positioning (Derivatives)

Instead of purely trusting Cash flow data, analyzing Futures & Options positioning gives you insight into the hedged direction professionals are betting on. DII footprint in options is minimal; FII rules this space.

  • Index Futures Ratio: Shows the sheer Long vs. Short contracts held by FIIs. A highly short-heavy position implies heavy hedging or bearish bets on benchmark indices like Nifty or Bank Nifty.
  • Options Long Calls: Technically a bullish position—buying the Right to purchase the index at higher dates.
  • Options Long Puts: Technically a bearish position—buying the Right to sell the index at lower levels. Extreme put writing/buying typically correlates with market corrections.
  • Momentum Sentiment Engine: A heuristic algorithm that weighs net futures, call options, and opposing put positions to summarize the net derivative bias (e.g., "Highly Bearish").

🗄️ 3. Historical Flow Databases & Matrices

A dedicated tabular data layer tracking institutional money across different timeframes. Features ultra-precise tabular-nums rendering for Bloomberg-terminal grade reading accuracy.

  • Daily (Last 15): Raw end-of-day gross buy, sell, and net numbers. Click "FII Bloodbath" or "DII Absorption" to rapidly filter structural anomaly days.
  • Data Export: Use the exact 'FROM' and 'TO' date selectors, and instantly jump out to a clean CSV via the "Download CSV" button for Excel modeling.
  • Weekly & Monthly Tracking: Groups aggregated capital to visualize broader macroeconomic trends pushing the Nifty index directionally.

📈 4. Historical Flow Trends (Interactive Charts)

Visualizing capital injection is easier than reading raw matrices. This tab relies on a highly responsive HTML5 Canvas engine to plot exact FII (Blue) and DII (Green) multi-axis bars.

  • Timeframe Toggles: Switch seamlessly between Daily, Weekly, Monthly, and Annual charting visualizers at the click of a button.
  • Visual Convergence: Whenever the Blue bar (FIIs) and Green bar (DIIs) move in tandem above the horizontal axis, expect explosive market rallies.

🌐 5. Sector Analytics (NSDL Data)

Published completely independent of NSE, this data tracks actual equity portfolios held by Foreign Portfolio Investors inside Indian Depositories via NSDL/CDSL. It is reported fortnightly.

  • Total AUM %: Identifies which 22 BSE sectors hold the majority of foreign stock. Financials universally dominate, followed by IT.
  • Fortnight Divergence (Δ): Extremely valuable for spotting Sector Rotation. Shows in exactly which sectors FPIs pumped or dumped capital over the last 15 days.
  • 1-Year Accumulation: Shows the long-term thematic stock picking of FPIs. Consistent positive numbers here usually mean major re-rating in broader sector multiples.
  • Interactive Charting: Expand the interactive diagram using the dropdown to visualize exact AUM percentage vs. 15-day buying intensity.
